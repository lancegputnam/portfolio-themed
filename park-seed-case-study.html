Park Seed

Working with the graphic designer for Park Seed, we are leading the initiative to redesign the web interface for the established brand. The previous layouts were crowded with text, images, heavy colors and gradients. We took the bones of the site and modernized them with less text and unnecessary marketing promos, stronger imagery and text, a simplified header and enlarged navigational menu and plenty of white space. After mocking up the site, I have begun hacking away at the markup and styling. The new design brings the website up to date visually and adds a sophisticated look look to the interface.

Discover Park Seed

Simplified Header

Leading the list in the redesign of Park Seed was simplifying the header. Most of the text that was previously in the header was consolidated in a dropdown under a new, My Account button. Centering the logo, making the search bar smaller and adding additional whitespace made the header modern and clean.

Navigation

The goal with the navigational menu of Park Seed was to make it easier to read. The layout was designed so that the user reads the content in an f-shape pattern. The width of the entire menu was also expanded, allowing for additional whitespace and better readability for the user.

Categories

The global design of the category pages was given updates to make the page more minimal and modern. The previously dark green background colors were swapped out for whites and soft greys. Marketing promos, which we found more distracting than useful, were also relocated, resulting in a layout that allowed more focus to be placed on the products.
